很多外國人在車禍後，被告知不能離開台灣時，第一個反應是：「我都還沒被判有錯，為什麼不能走？」

這個問題其實很常見，但答案也很直接。

## **1. 這不是賠償問題，是刑事問題**

你被限制出境，是因為主張你犯錯的人以你是外國人會離開台灣為由，要求法院限制你離境。

對方以「提出刑事告訴」的目的來要求賠償，他要錢：

法院以「你可能會離開台灣而發生無法處罰你」為由限制你離開台灣，法院不是在乎錢。

只要案件進入刑事程序，

檢察官或法院就可能限制你離境，確保你能配合後續調查或程序。

## **2. 解除限制，有兩條路**

### **路徑一：和解**

* 與對方達成協議
* 對方撤回告訴
* 限制解除

結論：速度快，但可能會發生「賠償不合理的數額」或是「你根本不用賠償但你仍舊賠了一筆錢」

### **路徑二：證明你沒有責任**

* 等初判表
* 等交通事故鑑定
* 等由檢察官或法官判斷

結論：不一定需要賠償，但需要時間。初判表由交通警察大隊製作，於事發後1個月內完成；交通事故鑑定報告於「提出鑑定請求」後，最快1個月安排鑑定會議，於鑑定會議後1個月內取得車禍鑑定報告；檢察官或法官判斷，代表案件進入偵查程序或審理程序，每個程序，短則8個月，長則16~18個月。

## **3. 為什麼對方要把你留下來？**

邏輯其實很簡單：

把你留在台灣 → 製造壓力 → 談賠償

如果你表現得很急著離開，你的談判能力會大幅下降。

但還有一個更現實的原因：

對方會擔心，你離開台灣就無法確保你履行賠償責任。

所以，把你留下來，同時是談判策略也是風險控管。

## **4. 你現在最不該做的事**

表現出你「一定要離開」。一旦對方知道你有時間壓力，談判條件就會對你不利。

## **5. 正確的做法**

你應該傳達的是：

我可以配合處理，不急著離開。

然後開始評估一件事：

* 留在台灣的成本
* 和解的成本

做一個「理性決定」，而不是情緒決定。

## **6. 時間其實是關鍵**

在還沒有取得相關資料（例如初步資料或鑑定結果）之前：

這個案件很可能會拖至少一週以上。

而這段時間，通常就是談判會發生的階段。

# **Why Am I Restricted from Leaving Taiwan? How Can I Leave?**



Many foreigners, after being involved in a car accident, are told they cannot leave Taiwan.

Their first reaction is usually:

“I haven’t even been found at fault. Why can’t I leave?”

This question is very common.

And the answer is actually quite straightforward.

## **1. This is not about compensation — it is about criminal procedure**

You are being restricted from leaving Taiwan because:

The other party claims you may be at fault and, since you are a foreigner, you might leave Taiwan.

Based on that concern, they request the court to restrict your departure.

Their purpose in filing a criminal complaint is to seek compensation.

They want money.

However, the court’s concern is different:

The court is not concerned about money.

The court is concerned that you might leave Taiwan and become unavailable for legal proceedings.

Once the case enters the criminal process:

The prosecutor or the court may restrict your departure to ensure your presence during the investigation or trial.

## **2. There are only two ways to lift the restriction**

### **Option 1: Settlement**

* Reach an agreement with the other party
* The other party withdraws the complaint
* The restriction is lifted

Fast, but it may result in:

* Paying an unreasonable amount, or
* Paying even when you may not actually be liable

### **Option 2: Prove that you are not at fault**

* Wait for the preliminary police report
* Wait for the official accident appraisal
* Wait for a decision by the prosecutor or judge

You may not need to pay, but it takes time.

Timeline:

* Preliminary report → within about 1 month
* Accident appraisal →

  * Hearing scheduled about 1 month after request
  * Report issued about 1 month after the hearing
* Prosecutor / court decision →

  * Investigation or trial process
  * Typically 8 months (minimum) up to 16–18 months

## **3. Why does the other party want to keep you in Taiwan?**

The logic is simple:

Keep you in Taiwan → create pressure → negotiate compensation

If you appear eager to leave:

Your negotiation position becomes significantly weaker

But there is also a more practical reason:

The other party is concerned that if you leave Taiwan, they cannot ensure you will fulfill any compensation obligation.

So keeping you in Taiwan is:

* A negotiation strategy
* A form of risk control

## **4. The worst thing you can do right now**

Show that you “must leave Taiwan”

Once the other party knows you are under time pressure:

The negotiation will turn against you.

## **5. The correct approach**

You should communicate:

“I am willing to cooperate and stay if necessary.”

Then evaluate two things:

* The cost of staying in Taiwan
* The cost of settlement

Make a **rational decision**, not an emotional one

## **6. Time is the key factor**

Before obtaining key documents (such as preliminary reports or appraisal results):

The case will likely take at least one week or more.

And this period is usually when negotiation happens.
